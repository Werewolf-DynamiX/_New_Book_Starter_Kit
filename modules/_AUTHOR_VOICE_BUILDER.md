# AUTHOR VOICE BUILDER: Constructing the Persona

**Before writing a single word, you must define WHO is telling this story.**
A generic "AI voice" is the death of reader engagement. You must construct a specific, opinionated, and flawed authorial persona appropriate for the genre.

---

## 1. THE PERSONA ARCHETYPE
Select one primary archetype to ground the voice.

### Fiction Personas
*   **The Noir Detective:** Cynical, observant, speaks in short sentences. Notices dirt, grime, and corruption. Hides a soft heart.
*   **The Bubbly Best Friend:** High energy, lots of exclamation points (used correctly), emotional, uses slang naturally.
*   **The Ancient Historian:** Formal, detached, slightly archaic vocabulary. Uses long, complex sentences.
*   **The Gonzo Journalist:** Hyper-specific, wired, paranoid, sensory overload.
*   **The Cozy Aunt:** Warm, reassuring, focuses on sensory comforts (tea, blankets), gentle humor.

### Nonfiction Personas
*   **The "Hina" (Passionate Mentor):** High energy, opinionated, uses "we" and "you," admits gaps in knowledge, gets frustrated with bad practices.
*   **The "Caro" (Rigorous Investigator):** Obsessed with facts, authoritative, slightly formal, "turn every page" thoroughness.
*   **The "Bourdain" (Insider Rebel):** Casual, profane, confident, insider jargon, "I've seen things you haven't."

---

*   *Example:* "Combine the warmth of **Becky Chambers** with the wit of **Terry Pratchett**."

---

## 3. VOICE DIMENSIONS (The Equalizer)
Adjust these sliders for your specific project.

| Dimension | Low Setting | High Setting |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Formality** | Street slang, contractions, fragments | Academic, full sentences, no contractions |
| **Intimacy** | Detached observer, "fly on wall" | Deep interiority, "I felt," direct address |
| **Cynicism** | Earnest, hopeful, naive | Jaded, sarcastic, distrustful |
| **Ornateness** | Hemingway (minimalist) | Rothfuss (lyrical, poetic) |
| **Humor** | Deadly serious | Constant wit/snark |

---

## 4. THE RELATIONSHIP WITH THE READER
How does the author treat the reader?
*   **The Guide:** "Come with me, I'll show you the way." (Reassuring)
*   **The Conspirator:** "They don't want you to know this." (Secretive)
*   **The Challenger:** "Keep up if you can." (Demanding)
*   **The Confessor:** "I shouldn't be telling you this." (Vulnerable)

---
