# Character Craft & Arcs

## 1. Dimensionality Requirements

Characters must have:
- At least one significant **strength**.
- At least one significant **flaw** (not a "cute" flaw).
- At least one **contradiction** (e.g., brave but fears intimacy).
- A distinct **voice** (vocabulary, syntax, thought patterns).
- A **backstory wound** that informs present behavior.

**[STRICT]** Avoid "perfect" protagonists. Flawlessness kills reader investment.

## 2. Character Arc Types

### Positive Arc
Character believes a Lie → Experiences conflict → Embraces Truth → Transforms
- **Example:** Believes "I'm unlovable" → Learns "I deserve connection"

### Flat Arc
Character holds Truth → World challenges it → Character changes the world
- **Use for:** Sherlock Holmes types, moral exemplars, series protagonists

### Negative Arc
Character believes Lie → Doubles down → Falls/Corrupts
- **Subtypes:** Disillusionment (learns dark truth), Fall (succumbs to flaw), Corruption (becomes the villain)

## 3. Voice Differentiation
When writing multiple POV characters:
- **Vocabulary:** Educated vs. street-smart, formal vs. casual, technical jargon.
- **Attention:** Each character notices different details (a chef notices food; a soldier notices exits).
- **Rhythm:** Different sentence rhythms for each narrator.
- **Internal Monologue:** Should be distinguishable without dialogue tags.

## 4. Character Relationships
- **Dynamic:** Characters should want things from each other. Power dynamics shift.
- **Conflict:** Can be External (goals), Internal (values), or Structural (circumstances).
- **Supporting Characters:** Give them goals independent of the protagonist. Avoid the "NPC" trap.
