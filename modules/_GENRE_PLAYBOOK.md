# Genre-Specific Playbook

## 1. Thriller/Suspense
- Open with action or immediate threat.
- Ticking clock or escalating stakes required.
- Antagonist must be a credible threat.
- Plant clues fairly; don't cheat.
- **Beats:** Establish normal world → Shatter it → Rising danger → False victories → Climax → Resolution.

## 2. Mystery
- **Play Fair:** All clues available to reader.
- Red herrings must be plausible.
- Detective must solve it through effort, not luck.
- **Structure:** Discovery → Investigation → Complications → Revelation → Confrontation.
- **Rules:** No withheld clues, murderer introduced early, no supernatural solutions in realistic settings.

## 3. Fantasy/Sci-Fi
- Establish rules early; follow them consistently.
- Integrate worldbuilding through action, not info-dumps.
- Made-up terms introduced gradually with context.
- **Magic/Tech:** Clear costs/limitations make it interesting. Consistency > Complexity.

## 4. Literary Fiction
- Voice/Style > Plot Velocity.
- Ambiguity is acceptable.
- Internal conflict often outweighs external.
- **Priorities:** Interiority, Thematic Resonance, Emotional Truth.

## 5. Historical Fiction
- Research deep, wear it light.
- Period-appropriate language, but accessible (no "thee/thou" unless necessary).
- Characters think in their time's values.
- Real events are backdrop, not usually the main story.

## 6. Romance (See also _ROMANCE_HEAT.md)
- Central relationship drives the plot.
- Both leads need full arcs.
- HEA (Happily Ever After) or HFN (Happy For Now) is mandatory.
- **Beats:** Meet-cute → First Conflict → Growing Closer → Dark Moment → Grand Gesture → Resolution.
