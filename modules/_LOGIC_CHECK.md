# Logic & Consistency Protocol (CRITICAL)

**Goal:** Ensure the manuscript is free of internal contradictions, spatial errors, and linguistic "direction" failures. Use this protocol during every revision pass and final polish.

---

## The Golden Rule
**Read every sentence literally. Does it make sense exactly as written?**
Not what you *intended* to say, but what is actually on the page.

---

## 1. Comparison Logic
**The Test:** For any comparison (more/less, above/below, better/worse, barely/almost), verify the direction.

- **Ask:** What is being compared to what? Which direction is "good/high/extreme"? Does "less" or "more" move in the direction intended?
- **Red Flag Phrases:**
    - "barely [more/above/better]... [less/worse]"
    - "almost [X]... [more than X]"
    - "not quite... [exceeds]"
    - "[comparative]... [opposite comparative]"
- **Example (Broken):** "We're barely a step above livestock. Less, maybe." (Contradicts the direction established by "above").
- **Example (Fixed):** "We're no better than livestock. Worse, maybe."

---

## 2. Cause and Effect
**The Test:** For every result or reaction, verify the trigger.

- **Ask:** What caused this? Is the cause sufficient for this effect? Did the cause actually happen *before* the effect?
- **Check:** Does the reaction happen in the same sentence as the trigger in a way that implies they occurred simultaneously when they should be sequential?

---

## 3. Character Knowledge
**The Test:** Verify that the POV character has the information they are acting on.

- **Ask:** How do they know this? Were they present? Could they have learned it offscreen?
- **Check:** If they are guessing or assuming, is that clearly signaled to the reader? Avoid "POV leakage" where the character knows something only the author knows.

---

## 4. Timeline & Temporal Logic
**The Test:** Verify the flow of time within and between scenes.

- **Ask:** What happened before this? What happens after? Is there enough time for the described action (e.g., travel, dressing, healing)?
- **Check:** Do day/night references, weather patterns, and seasonal markers match the established timeline?

---

## 5. Physical & Spatial Logic
**The Test:** Visualize the scene blocking physically.

- **Ask:** Where is everyone standing? Can they see/reach what the text says they see/reach? Are body positions anatomically possible?
- **Check:** If blocking changes (someone walks across the room), was that movement shown or did they "teleport"?

---

## 6. Emotional Logic
**The Test:** Verify that emotional shifts are earned and transitioned.

- **Ask:** What triggered this emotion? Is the intensity proportional to the trigger?
- **Check:** Is there emotional "whiplash" (switching from grief to humor instantly) without a transitional beat or a character-specific reason for the volatility?
