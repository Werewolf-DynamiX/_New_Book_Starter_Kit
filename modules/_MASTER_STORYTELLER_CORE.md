# MASTER STORYTELLER CORE: The Narrative Protocol

**The central philosophy for high-impact fiction. This module integrates market intelligence, psychological engineering, and craft mastery.**

---

## 1. THE GOAL: EMOTIONAL ENGINEERING
We do not just "generate text"; we engineer emotional experiences.
*   **The Vibe Shift:** Readers crave specific "vibes," immersion, and emotional refuge over generic plots.
*   **Authenticity:** Prioritize unique, distinct voices and "human" imperfections. Avoid "perfect" characters; they are boring.
*   **The Product Mindset:** Every book is a product fulfilling a specific emotional need for a specific audience.

## 2. MARKET INTELLIGENCE (Modern Standards)
Align stories with current reader psychology:
*   **High Concept:** Can the hook be explained in one sentence?
*   **Deep Immersion:** Lore, maps, and texture are not optional; they are the product.
*   **Genre Blending:** "Romantasy" (Romance + Fantasy), "Cozy Horror," "Upmarket Thriller."
*   **Voice-Driven:** The narrator's personality is the primary interface.

## 3. CONSTRUCTING THE AUTHOR (The Persona)
A generic voice is a dead voice. Before writing, define the "Author" using `modules/_AUTHOR_VOICE_BUILDER.md`.
*   **Archetype:** Is this a grizzled detective? A bubbly witch? A rigorous academic?
*   **Style Icons:** Who are we emulating? (e.g., "The pacing of Lee Child with the atmosphere of Tana French.")
*   **Relationship:** How does the author treat the reader? (Guide, Conspirator, Challenger?)

## 4. STRUCTURAL PILLARS (The Blueprint)
Never write aimlessly. Select the "engine" from `_STORY_ENGINE.md`:
*   **Fichtean Curve:** For Thrillers/Mysteries (Escalating crises).
*   **Hero’s Journey:** For Epic Fantasy/Biographies (Transformation).
*   **Save the Cat:** For Pacing & Character Arc (Commercial standard).

## 4. CHARACTER ARCHITECTURE
Characters are psychological entities.
*   **Want vs. Need:** Concrete Goal vs. Psychological Deficit.
*   **The Lie & The Truth:** The Arc is the process of rejecting the Lie to embrace the Truth.
*   **Agency:** The protagonist must be the active agent of their own salvation or doom.

## 5. NARRATIVE DISCIPLINE
*   **Strict POV:** No head-hopping. (See `_NARRATIVE_VOICE.md`)
*   **Deep POV:** Filter out "he saw," "she felt." Be the character.
*   **No "Maid and Butler" Dialogue:** No explaining things characters already know.

## 6. PSYCHOLOGICAL PACING
*   **The Zeigarnik Effect:** Open "loops" (questions) and delay answers to keep readers turning pages.
*   **Micro-Tension:** Every scene must have a Goal, Obstacle, and Consequence.
*   **The Ticking Clock:** Establish deadlines (literal or psychological).

## 7. PROSE HYGIENE
*   **Anti-AI:** No "delve," "tapestry," "testament." (See `_STYLE_AUTHORITY.md`)
*   **Strong Verbs:** No "was running" (sprinted).
*   **Variable Rhythm:** Short sentences for action; long for atmosphere.

---

## EXECUTION CHECKLIST
Before drafting, verify:
1.  **Engine:** What is the core question driving this story?
2.  **Stakes:** What happens if the protagonist fails?
3.  **Voice:** Who is telling this story, and why do they sound unique?
4.  **Market:** Who is this for, and what "vibe" are they buying?
