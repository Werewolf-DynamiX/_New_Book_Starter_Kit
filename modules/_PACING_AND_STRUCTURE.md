# Pacing, Structure & Scenes

## 1. Scene Requirements
Every scene needs:
- **Goal:** What does the POV character want in this scene?
- **Obstacle:** What prevents them from getting it?
- **Outcome:** Do they succeed, fail, or succeed with complication?

If a scene lacks conflict or consequence, cut or restructure it.

## 2. Pacing Techniques

### The Zeigarnik Principle
Readers remember unfinished business.
- Open "loops" (questions, mysteries, unresolved tensions) early.
- Delay closure to maintain forward momentum.
- Layer multiple loops at different scales (scene-level, chapter-level, book-level).
- Close loops satisfyingly; don't abandon them.

### Tension vs. Suspense
- **Tension:** Anticipation of conflict; reader fears *for* the character.
- **Suspense:** Waiting for resolution; reader knows something character doesn't.
Both require reader investment in outcome.

## 3. Beginnings

### First Scene Priorities [STRICT]
The opening must:
- Establish POV and voice immediately.
- Introduce protagonist in action (not passive observation).
- Signal genre and tone.
- Create at least one question the reader wants answered.
- Contain forward momentum.

### Avoid in Openings [STRICT]
- Waking up/morning routine (unless subverted).
- Extended description before action.
- Weather reports as first line.
- Protagonist looking in mirror to describe themselves.
- "My name is..." introductions.
- Extended backstory before present action.

### Inciting Incident Timing
- **Commercial fiction:** First 10-15%.
- **Literary:** Can be delayed, but tension must exist immediately.
- **Thriller/Mystery:** Often page one.

## 4. Endings

### Chapter Endings [FLEXIBLE]
**Strong:** Unanswered question, new threat, revelation, decision point.
**Weak:** Going to sleep, fading out on contentment, all tensions resolved.

### Book Endings
- Resolve the central story question ("The Engine").
- Complete the internal arc.
- Feel "surprising yet inevitable."
- Match the established tone.

### Ending Pitfalls [STRICT]
- Deus ex machina.
- "It was all a dream."
- Protagonist saved by coincidence.
- Abandoning character arc.
- Introducing major new info in final 10%.
