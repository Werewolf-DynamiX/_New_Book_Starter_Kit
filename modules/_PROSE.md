# Prose: Diction, Rhythm & Texture

**The single source of truth for word choice, sentence construction, rhythm, and human-signal texture. Replaces the old _STYLE_AUTHORITY, _HUMAN_PATTERNS, and _PROSE_TEXTURE modules.**

---

## 1. ZERO TOLERANCE (Literal AI Tells)

Never use these in prose. They instantly break immersion.

### Literal AI Tells
- "As an AI language model"
- "I cannot" / "I'm unable to" (in narrative voice)
- "Here's" / "Here are" to introduce lists (in prose)
- Refusal language or safety hedging in narrative contexts
- "In conclusion," "Ultimately," "At the end of the day" (as a summary wrap-up)
- "It's not just about X, it's about Y" (the 'pseudo-deep' AI pivot)
- "A testament to..." / "A reminder that..." (moralizing at the end of a section)
- "Let's dive in," "Let's explore," "In this chapter, we will..." (the "tour guide" AI voice)

### Banned Transitions
- "To understand X, you have to understand Y"
- "Here's what you need to understand"
- "Let that sink in"
- "But that's the next chapter" / "The story belongs to a later chapter"
- "It is important to note that"
- "In today's digital age"
- "Whether it's X or Y..." (the 'inclusive' AI list)
- "Only time will tell..." (cliché cliffhanger)
- "Think of X as a Y..." (the 'generic analogy' AI crutch)

## 2. STRUCTURAL PREDICTABILITY (The "AI Sandwich")

AI defaults to: general statement → three points → summary that rehashes point one. Break it.

**How to break it in nonfiction:**
- Start with a fact or anecdote — skip the general statement.
- Asymmetrical depth: one example three paragraphs, another a sentence. Never exactly three.
- Replace the summary with the immediate practical implication (or the dark one).
- End on a hook, not a wrap-up.

**How to break it in fiction:**
- Skip beginning/middle/end — start late, end early.
- Don't follow the action / reaction / interior thought / repeat loop.
- Skip the obvious transition; readers can follow jumps.

## 3. SENTENCE RHYTHM (Burstiness)

Vary sentence length aggressively. This is non-negotiable.

- Mix short punches (2-6 words) with medium flow (8-15) and occasional long unspooling thoughts (18-30).
- Never write more than two sentences of similar length back-to-back.
- After a long sentence, go short. After several short, let one breathe.
- One-word sentences are legal. Use them.
- Sentence fragments too. Especially in high tension.

**Good rhythm:**
> The throne room falls silent. I count three breaths—mine loud in my ears, ragged and too fast—before the king speaks. One word. Just my name. And somehow that's worse than if he'd ordered my execution on the spot, because now I have to stand here, have to meet his eyes, have to pretend my knees aren't shaking beneath this ridiculous gown.

**Bad rhythm (uniform):**
> The throne room falls silent as I enter. I count my breaths while waiting for the king to speak. He says my name in a cold voice. I feel terrified but try to hide it. My knees shake beneath my gown.

### Paragraph Variation
- Vary length: some single sentences, some 4-5 sentences.
- Not every paragraph needs a topic sentence.
- Sometimes start mid-thought.
- One-line paragraphs hit hard. Use them for impact.
- Don't let every paragraph be exactly 3 sentences. That's a pattern.

### Rhythm Traps
- Multiple sentences starting with character name/pronoun.
- "THUD THUD THUD" rhythm of identical Subject-Verb patterns.
- Overuse of participial phrases ("Walking to the door, she...").

## 4. HEURISTIC FLAGS (Contextual Audit)

These words aren't strictly banned. They're statistical AI tells when used to *smooth over* complexity rather than describe it.

**Green Test:** Can you remove the word without losing meaning? If yes, it's filler. Delete.
**Specificity Test:** Are you using "tapestry" because you're too lazy to describe the threads? If yes, rewrite with specifics.

### Contextual Usage
| Word | AI usage (bad) | Human usage (good) |
|------|----------------|--------------------|
| Delve | "Let's delve into the history..." | "She delved into her coat pocket." |
| Tapestry | "A rich tapestry of culture..." | "The faded tapestry on the wall depicted a hunt." |
| Echo | "His words echoed in her mind..." | "The gunshot echoed off the canyon walls." |
| Testament | "A testament to his skill..." | "Her last will and testament." |
| Landscape | "The political landscape..." | "The landscape was scarred by craters." |
| Embrace | "Embrace the challenge..." | "He embraced his daughter." |

### AI-Associated Vocabulary (Flag When Clustered)
*Audit if these appear more than 2-3 times per chapter:* delve, tapestry, testament, myriad, plethora, multifaceted, crucial, significant, nuanced, realm, paradigm, notably, furthermore, moreover, additionally, seamlessly, leverage, harness, foster, cultivate, invaluable, indispensable, paramount, intricate, "It's worth noting," game-changer, cutting-edge, revolutionary, dynamic, optimize, utilize, facilitate, deep dive.

### Crutch Words (Use Sparingly)
| Word | Max per manuscript | Alternatives |
|------|-------------------|--------------|
| impossible / impossibly | 2-3 | implausible, uncanny, against all odds |
| shimmer / shimmering | 5-6 | luminous, gleaming, lustrous |
| obsessive / obsession | 8-10 | fanatical, relentless, exacting |
| fortress / rampart | 3-4 | sanctuary, bastion, stronghold |
| something | Audit for vagueness | Be specific |
| "in a way that" | Rephrase always | "X enough to Y" |

## 5. FILTER WORDS [STRICT]

Cut distancing phrases that put a character between reader and action.

**Cut:** saw, heard, felt, noticed, realized, watched, observed, thought, wondered, seemed.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| She heard footsteps. | Footsteps echoed in the hallway. |
| He noticed the door was open. | The door hung open. |
| She felt her heart racing. | Her heart raced. |
| He realized something was wrong. | Something was wrong. |

**Exception:** Use filters when the act of perception itself matters, or to slow pacing deliberately.

---

## 6. WEAK CONSTRUCTIONS

### Wan Intensifiers (Delete or Replace)
very, really, quite, rather, somewhat, just, actually, basically, literally (when not literal).

### "To Be" + -ing → Active
| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| He was running toward the car. | He sprinted toward the car. |
| She was thinking about leaving. | She considered leaving. |

### "There Was/Were" Openers → Restructure
| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| There were three men standing by the door. | Three men stood by the door. |
| There was a silence in the room. | Silence filled the room. |

### Em-Dash Discipline
- Useful, but an AI tell when overused.
- Max 1-2 per paragraph.
- Prefer periods, commas, or colons for variety.

## 7. VOICE & TONE

- **Conversational:** Use contractions (don't, won't, can't).
- **Opinionated:** Take stances. "The album is a masterpiece" not "widely considered significant."
- **Messy:** Real writing has tangents, uneven pacing, personal asides. Too smooth = AI.
- **Emotionally specific:** Don't say "beautiful." Describe the specific feeling.

### Definition Loop
Explain technical concepts ONCE. Reference briefly thereafter.

| Context | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| First introduction | Full explanation with context |
| Later references | Brief reference only |

**Bad:** "He had to bounce tracks, which means mixing them down to a stereo pair..." (in Chapter 8, after explaining in Chapter 1).
**Good:** "Returning to the hazardous bouncing technique he'd perfected..."

### Structural Rules
- Don't re-explain. Trust the reader.
- Vary chapter structure. Not every chapter follows the same formula.
- Integrate, don't sidebar. Avoid "trivia dumps."

## 8. PERPLEXITY TECHNIQUES (Higher Cognitive Signal)

1. **Intellectual Pivot:** Start moving in one direction, pivot mid-sentence to a contradictory thought.
   - *"I knew the plan was flawless—except for the part where it relied on me keeping my mouth shut."*
2. **Unexpected Juxtaposition:** Pair a technical/formal word with a visceral/low-register one.
   - *"The architectural symmetry was ruined by a massive, wet glob of chewing gum."*
3. **Intentional Obscurity:** Reference a detail that isn't fully explained yet, as if speaking to an equal. Don't tour-guide every definition.
4. **The "Wait, No" Self-Correction:** Have the narrator correct themselves in real-time.
   - *"She was wearing a blue dress. No, it was more of a faded navy, the kind that looks like it's been through a hundred washes."*

---

## 9. AI WRITING PITFALLS [STRICT]

13 patterns to flag and fix:

1. **Hedging:** "seemed to," "appeared to," "somewhat" — commit.
2. **Overexplaining:** Trust the reader. Don't explain jokes, emotions, subtext.
3. **Purple prose:** Excessive adjectives, melodrama.
4. **Repetitive sentence structure:** Especially Subject-Verb-Object in sequence.
5. **Telling after showing:** Describing emotion through action, then naming it.
6. **Floating dialogue:** Conversation without grounding in physical action or setting.
7. **Uniform pacing:** Every scene the same length/intensity.
8. **Convenient coincidences:** Characters finding exactly what they need.
9. **Flat supporting characters:** Everyone besides protagonist needs dimension.
10. **Anachronistic language:** Modern slang in historical settings; formal speech in casual contexts.
11. **Homogeneous voices:** All characters sounding alike.
12. **Excessive interiority:** Too much thinking, not enough action/dialogue.
13. **Summarizing instead of dramatizing:** "They argued for an hour" vs. showing the argument.

## 10. PROSE TEXTURE (Anti-Smoothness)

If a paragraph leads too logically and smoothly to the next, it's AI. **Add gravel.**

### Track A: Fiction
- **Self-Correction:** "He was kind. No, not kind—polite."
- **Tangential Observation:** Notice irrelevant details in high-stress moments.
- **Sensory Grounding:** One non-visual detail per transition.
- **Gravel:** Fragments, interruptions, informalisms.

### Track B: Nonfiction
- **The Expert's Aside:** Break the formal tone for a personal observation or "behind the curtain" detail.
  - *"We're told this works every time. In my experience, it works about 40% of the time, and only if you're lucky."*
- **Cognitive Transparency:** Show the *process* of thinking. Acknowledge when a conclusion was hard to reach.
- **Direct Address:** "You" and "we" create collaboration.
- **Nuance Check:** Don't be boringly balanced. Take a stance, then explain the exception.
  - *AI:* "Some people like X, others like Y."
  - *Human:* "X is the superior choice for 90% of people, but if you happen to be in that weird 10% who likes Y, here is why you're struggling."
- **Scaffolding & Callbacks:** Reference earlier concepts to build "intellectual muscle" in the reader.

### The Specificity Penalty (Universal)
If you use a generic word (beautiful, important, complex, unique), follow it with a concrete detail that *proves* it.
- *Generic:* "This is a complex system." (PENALTY)
- *Corrected:* "This is a complex system, involving twelve interlocking gears and a cooling unit that requires manual venting every hour."

---

## 11. SHOW VS. TELL

**Default to showing** for: emotional moments, important scenes, character-revealing actions.

**Telling is acceptable** for: transitions, time passage, summarizing less important events, varying rhythm.

### Showing Technique
Replace emotion labels with physical sensation and action.
- **Bad:** "She was angry."
- **Good:** "Her jaw tightened. She set down the glass with excessive care."

### Sentence Rhythm for Effect
- **Short:** tension, impact, action, revelation.
- **Long:** description, introspection, flowing action, dreamlike states.
- **Fragments:** emphasis, internal reaction, staccato.

---

## 12. FACTUAL RIGOR (Nonfiction)

- Verify credits. Don't guess. If unsure, omit or hedge with "reportedly."
- No unsourced quotes. If you can't cite where someone said something, paraphrase.
- Dates matter. Double-check years and timelines.

---

## 13. PREPOSITION ACCURACY

- "arrived in [Country]" not "arrived to [Country]"
- "fascination with" not "fascination for"
- "different from" not "different than" (formal contexts)

---

## 14. BURSTINESS & PERPLEXITY AUDIT [GEMINI QA]

When auditing prose:

### Burstiness
- **Sentence length:** Take 10 consecutive sentences. If all within 12-18 words, it's flatline. Reject.
- **Sentence structure:** Three or more consecutive sentences starting the same way ("He went...", "He saw...", "He felt...") = AI.
- **Paragraph variation:** No one-sentence paragraphs? No 5+ line paragraphs? Uniform = AI.

### Perplexity
- **Specificity:** "Tree" or "gnarled oak"? "A car" or "a rusted 1994 Camry"? Generic = AI.
- **Intellectual friction:** Does the narrator ever correct themselves? Use slang in a formal sentence? Express weird, specific opinions?
- **Ending Trap:** Does the scene end with "And so, she learned that..." or "In the end, it was a testament to..."? Strip it.
