# Story Engine & Narrative Architecture

## 1. Core Principles

### 1.1 Story Engine
Every story needs a clear engine—the core question or tension that drives the narrative forward:
- **Mystery:** Who did it? What happened?
- **Romance:** Will they get together?
- **Thriller:** Will they survive/succeed?
- **Literary:** Who is this person, really?

Identify the engine before drafting. Every scene should either advance the engine or develop character—preferably both.

### 1.2 Stakes & Weight
- The protagonist must have something meaningful to lose.
- Stakes can be external (life, safety, relationships) or internal (identity, beliefs, self-worth).
- Escalate stakes progressively; don't peak too early.

### 1.3 The Want/Need Framework
Every protagonist requires:
- **Want:** A concrete external goal (find the killer, win the competition, get the job).
- **Need:** An internal psychological deficit to heal (learn to trust, overcome shame, accept mortality).

The story's emotional resonance comes from the tension between these two.

## 2. Structure Templates

Select based on genre and user preference. Default to **Three-Act** if unspecified.

### 2.1 Three-Act Structure (Default)
- **Act 1 (25%):** Setup, establish normal world, inciting incident, first plot point.
- **Act 2 (50%):** Confrontation, rising complications, midpoint shift, dark moment.
- **Act 3 (25%):** Resolution, climax, denouement.

### 2.2 Fichtean Curve (Thrillers/Mysteries)
- Skip extended exposition.
- Open *in media res* with immediate crisis.
- Structure as escalating crises (mini-climaxes) leading to main climax.
- Each crisis raises stakes higher than the last.

### 2.3 Save the Cat Beats (Commercial Fiction)
Key beats to hit:
1. Opening Image
2. Setup (1-10%)
3. Catalyst/Inciting Incident (10%)
4. Debate (10-20%)
5. Break into Act 2 (20%)
6. B-Story introduction
7. Fun and Games (20-50%)
8. Midpoint (50%)—false victory or false defeat
9. Bad Guys Close In (50-75%)
10. All Is Lost (75%)
11. Dark Night of the Soul
12. Break into Act 3 (80%)
13. Finale
14. Final Image (mirrors/contrasts Opening Image)

### 2.4 Hero's Journey (Transformation/Epic)
Use for stories emphasizing internal transformation:
Ordinary World → Call to Adventure → Refusal → Meeting the Mentor → Crossing the Threshold → Tests/Allies/Enemies → Approach → Ordeal → Reward → Road Back → Resurrection → Return with Elixir
