# Word Variation & Crutch Discipline

## Crutch Words to Limit (USE SPARINGLY)
These are fine occasionally but become AI tells when overused:

- **"impossible" / "impossibly"** — max 2-3 per manuscript
- **"shimmer" / "shimmering"** — max 5-6 per manuscript
- **"obsessive" / "obsession"** — max 8-10 per manuscript
- **"fortress" / "rampart"** — max 3-4 per manuscript
- **"something"** — audit for vagueness, be specific instead
- **"in a way that"** — rephrase to be direct

## Em-Dash Discipline
- Em-dashes (—) are useful but become an AI tell when overused
- **Rule:** Max 1-2 per paragraph
- Prefer periods, commas, or colons for variety
- Never use em-dashes for every parenthetical aside

## Word Variation Cheat Sheet
When you catch yourself repeating, use these alternatives:

**Instead of "obsessive/obsession":**
- fanatical, relentless, meticulous, exacting, devoted, uncompromising, perfectionist, driven

**Instead of "shimmer/shimmering":**
- luminous, glassy, gleaming, lustrous, crystalline, radiant, glow

**Instead of "fortress/rampart":**
- sanctuary, refuge, bastion, stronghold, haven, retreat, walls

**Instead of "impossible":**
- implausible, uncanny, otherworldly, ethereal, improbable, against all odds

**Instead of "in a way that":**
- Rephrase entirely. "X in a way that Y" → "X, something that Y" or "X enough to Y" or just restructure.

## Preposition Accuracy
- "arrived in [Country]" not "arrived to [Country]"
- "fascination with" not "obsession with" (when varying)
- "recognized potential she hadn't yet realized" not "recognized potential in her that she hadn't fully realized herself"

## Weak Constructions to Avoid

### Wan Intensifiers—Delete or Replace
These weaken prose. Cut them or find stronger words:
- very, really, quite, rather, somewhat, just, actually, basically, literally (when not literal)

### "To Be" + -ing Constructions—Convert to Active Verbs
- **Weak:** "He was running toward the car."
- **Strong:** "He sprinted toward the car."

### "There Was/There Were" Openers
- **Weak:** "There were three men standing by the door."
- **Strong:** "Three men stood by the door."
